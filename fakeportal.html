TBA The Fake Portal was first announced by RealLifeFakeWizard in a YouTube video on July 14, 2025, during which the creator outlined intentions to open a database website supporting indie developers and opposing AAA publishers and corporations. YouTube announcement by RealLifeFakeWizard ArgonianVoter, a user on KiwiFarms, characterized the project on July 7, 2025, as a program funded to compete with IGN in order to support indie developers against corporate dominance; the post noted a newsletter signup at fakeportal.com to notify users when the site launches and promised searchable tags detailing company philosophies, team sizes, and budgets. KiwiFarms post by ArgonianVoter GodwayGames promoted the project on itch.io on July 16, 2025, featuring a beta preview screenshot and describing an IGN-style interface exclusively for indie game listings, explicitly excluding entries from major publishers such as EA and Ubisoft. itch.io promotion Planned features under ongoing discussion include quality screening to filter out low-effort or non-original submissions, a tagging and metadata system covering genres, platforms, team sizes, and accessibility options, advanced search and filter capabilities by engine and release status, and community-driven edit histories with moderation queues. The domain fakeportal.com is registered; however, as of July 18, 2025, it does not host a publicly accessible prototype or documentation pending public release.

Project Status

  • Beta phase; public launch date not yet announced.
  • Active design and governance discussions occur on the “Wizard Sanctuary” Discord server (discord.gg/eXna6fd8Yg).

Considerations for Inclusion

  • Scope: Only games developed by independent teams are eligible for listing.
  • Curation: Quality control measures aim to prevent asset flips and shovelware.
  • Metadata: Detailed tagging covers genres, platforms, team sizes, and accessibility features.
